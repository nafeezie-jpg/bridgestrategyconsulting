About

Some people study the world from one side of the wall. She has always worked from the crack in the middle.

Between the engineer and the farmer. Between the donor and the beneficiary. Between the data and the human being behind it. Between the ancient manuscript and the modern reader.

Her work is translation. Not the linguistic kind, but the deeper kind, where expertise becomes accessible, strategy becomes action, and the people most affected by a system finally have someone in the room who speaks both languages fluently.

Formed across Nigeria, the United States, and the United Kingdom, holding four degrees including a PhD in Development Economics, Nafeesat carries eight years of high-stakes execution and an unwavering conviction that the gap between institutional power and human need is never an accident. It is a failure of translation. And translation is what she does.

PhD Research · University of East Anglia · 2025

The Aid-for-Trade Paradox

Doctoral research that named and evidenced a paradox at the heart of global aid policy. Drawing on an original dataset built from scratch, trade cost analysis, export performance data, gravity modelling, and direct fieldwork with respondents across Nigeria, the research revealed that aid simultaneously increases trade costs and improves export performance, a finding with direct implications for how billions in development finance are structured and deployed.
